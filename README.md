# THE CITIZEN’S SHIELD

**A Framework for Restoring Constitutional Accountability Through Modernized Citizen’s Arrest Authority and Mandatory Public Body Camera Disclosure**

-----

> *“The poorest man may in his cottage bid defiance to all the force of the crown.”*
> — William Pitt, 1763

-----

## Overview

The founding generation built two interlocking safeguards against government tyranny into the American legal framework: the Fourth Amendment’s prohibition on unreasonable searches and seizures, and the common law right of citizens to arrest government agents who commit crimes in their presence.

Over two and a half centuries, both safeguards have been gutted. Qualified immunity shields officers from civil liability. Body camera footage is routinely withheld behind “ongoing investigation” exemptions that never expire. And citizen’s arrest authority has been either repealed, narrowed to irrelevance, or weaponized for purposes the founders never intended.

**The Citizen’s Shield** proposes a three-part framework to restore the constitutional balance:

1. **Mandatory, non-discretionary public release** of all police body camera footage within a fixed statutory window, with narrow, judicially reviewable exceptions.
1. **Modernized citizen’s arrest authority** specifically restructured for its original purpose: empowering citizens to detain government agents observed committing crimes under color of authority.
1. **An evidentiary bridge** between the two: body camera footage as the objective, tamper-resistant record that protects both the citizen making the arrest and the officer being arrested from false claims.

-----

## The Problem

Three developments destroyed the original constitutional balance:

- **Professional police forces replaced citizen policing.** When the founders wrote the Constitution, there were no police departments. The first modern American police force didn’t exist until half a century later. Arrest authority transferred from citizens to the state.
- **Qualified immunity was invented.** Not in the Constitution. Not in any statute passed by Congress. A judge-made doctrine (Pierson v. Ray, 1967; Harlow v. Fitzgerald, 1982) that in practice functions as near-absolute immunity for officers.
- **Citizen’s arrest laws were weaponized or repealed.** Most notoriously in the 2020 killing of Ahmaud Arbery. Georgia repealed its citizen’s arrest law in 2021. New York, Florida, and South Carolina have repeal efforts underway.

The impulse to repeal is understandable. But the repeal movement throws out the baby with the bathwater. Citizen’s arrest was never designed for private citizens to chase suspected shoplifters. It was designed as a check on government power.

**The answer is not abolition. It is restructuring.**

## Framework Components

### Part 1: Mandatory Public Body Camera Disclosure

|Requirement                   |Standard                                                                                           |
|------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Release window**            |72 hours from encounter                                                                            |
|**Minor privacy**             |Faces blurred; encounter still disclosed                                                           |
|**Active tactical operation** |Up to 14-day delay; court order required beyond; 90-day maximum                                    |
|**Sexual assault / DV victim**|Victim identity redacted; officer conduct still disclosed                                          |
|**Retention**                 |3-year minimum; permanent for use of force, arrest, complaint, injury, or death                    |
|**Tamper protection**         |Auto-upload to third-party repository within 24 hours                                              |
|**Activation mandate**        |All public encounters, no exceptions; failure is disciplinary; intentional deactivation is criminal|

**What is NOT an exception:** “Ongoing investigation,” officer privacy, departmental review, union grievance proceedings.

### Part 2: Modernized Citizen’s Arrest Authority

**Scope:** Applies *exclusively* to the detention of government agents observed committing criminal acts under color of authority. This is not a general citizen’s arrest authority.

**Five elements required:**

1. **Witnessed criminal conduct** — personally observed acts constituting a criminal offense (assault, unlawful entry, unlawful search, evidence tampering, etc.)
1. **Contemporaneous recording** — citizen recording via personal device, or capture on the officer’s mandatorily-activated body camera
1. **Verbal declaration** — citizen states the arrest, the specific conduct observed, and that independent law enforcement is being summoned
1. **Immediate notification** — contact a law enforcement agency *other than* the arrested agent’s employer to take custody
1. **Minimum force** — only what is necessary to prevent flight; deadly force never authorized except in lawful self-defense

### Part 3: The Evidentiary Bridge

The two proposals form a closed accountability loop:

```
Body cameras record → Citizen witnesses misconduct → Citizen exercises arrest authority
→ Recording protects both parties → Independent agency takes custody
→ Public has access to footage (72-hr release) → Qualified immunity does not apply
```

Without mandatory disclosure, citizen’s arrest is he-said-she-said. Without citizen’s arrest authority, mandatory disclosure is observation without consequence. **Together, they create a closed loop.**

## Protections

### For the Citizen

- **Retaliation prohibition** with enhanced penalties for false charges, unfounded investigations, or harassment
- **Qualified immunity inapplicability** — the arrested agent cannot assert qualified immunity in subsequent proceedings
- **Good faith protection** — civil immunity for arrests made in good faith with recording and substantial procedural compliance
- **Adverse evidentiary presumption** — if mandatory body camera footage doesn’t exist, the presumption favors the citizen’s account

### For the Officer

- **Recording requirement** documents every citizen’s arrest — fabricated arrests are immediately disproven
- **Independent agency custody** — the citizen does not transport, interrogate, or process the officer
- **Good faith performance defense** — lawful conduct shown on recording defeats the citizen’s arrest and removes the citizen’s good faith protection

|**First**     |Right to record police activity in public (established by multiple federal circuits)           |
|**Fourteenth**|Due process protections for officers via independent-agency-custody requirement                |
|**Tenth**     |Citizen’s arrest authority as a matter of state law; implementable without federal legislation |

## Relationship to Qualified Immunity

This framework does **not** require abolishing qualified immunity generally. It carves a specific, narrow, evidence-based exception: qualified immunity does not apply when a government agent’s criminal conduct was (a) witnessed by a citizen, (b) contemporaneously recorded, and (c) the subject of a lawful citizen’s arrest under this framework.

This does not expose officers to liability for split-second judgment calls. It exposes officers to liability for **criminal conduct that was recorded happening.**

## Current Legislative Context (2026)

|Legislation                                        |Direction                                              |
|---------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
|Qualified Immunity Act of 2025 (S.122 / H.R.503)   |Codify and strengthen qualified immunity               |
|Qualified Immunity Abolition Act of 2026 (H.R.7046)|Abolish for federal officers                           |
|Ending Qualified Immunity Act (Pressley/Markey)    |Eliminate for state and local officials                |
|Police CAMERA Act of 2025 (H.R.1188)               |Grants for body cameras (no disclosure mandate)        |
|FLESA Act (Booker)                                 |Minimum standards and body cameras for federal officers|

The Citizen’s Shield occupies a **middle position**: it does not abolish qualified immunity across the board, but removes it in the specific circumstance where misconduct is recorded and a citizen has exercised structured arrest authority.

## Implementation

**State level (model statute):** Mandatory activation → 72-hour disclosure → tamper-proof storage → modernized citizen’s arrest → retaliation prohibition → qualified immunity exception → adverse presumption for missing footage.

**Federal level:** Applies to ICE, CBP, FBI, DEA, ATF, USMS, Secret Service. Amends FOIA exemptions for law enforcement records.

**Pilot jurisdictions:** Colorado or New Mexico — both have existing strong body camera mandates and have already eliminated qualified immunity at the state level.
